A bit about my art...

My painting journey began only a few years ago, when I started taking a local tole painting class with my mom. That was done with acrylic paint and pre-written and pre-drawn patterns and step-by-step instructions. Although it may sound a bit like paint by number, is was actually quite difficult and I needed more help than I realized. There was a lot more to it than just painting colors into a shape. There was layering, there was blending, there was floating and shading, there was a specific way to load the brush with paint. And I loved every minute of it. That was my first step towards becoming an artist.

I then slowly began adding on to my repertoire and eventually picked up watercolor and instantly fell in love. Watercolor pairs well with mixed media formats, and so I quickly added gouache and pen & ink to my ever-growing list of mediums.

I am a learner by nature. Once I learn the basics, I need to learn more. The same happened with watercolor. It wasn't enough that I could paint with it (which I still can't, but I enjoy it!). I soon found myself wanted to learn more about color theory and delving deeper into the world of pigments and what each number means on a tube of paint. I wanted to learn more about fugitive colors, cool and warm colors, the history of these colors - anything and everything I could get my hands on.

    A few of my favorite supplies

  • Brushes
    • Princeton Aqua Elite

    These brushes are made specifically to hold water. They are made from a synthetic hair that mimics natural Kolinsky. These are professional level brushes that are affordable

    Where to buy: Dick Blick Art Materials, Jerry's Artarama

  • Paper

    • High quality watercolor paper is best because it allows the paint to be manipulated without putting stress on the paper. This includes blending, lifting, layering, etc. Choosing a 100% cotton watercolor paper is the best way to ensure your paints look their best.

    There are several ways to purchase paper. It can be purchased in the form of a sketchbook, loose or a watercolor block. A watercolor block is a pad of paper glued on all sides with a corner or a small section unglued so you can remove the paper when your painting is finished.

    I use: Fabriano Artistico, Legion Stonehenge

  • Paint
    • M. Graham

    M. Graham watercolors are my absolute favorite. The formula is highly pigmented and is very smooth. There is a wide range of colors to choose from, and lots of granulating options. The honey-based formula creates a paint that blends and mixes beautifully. The paints are easily rewetted and come in tubes that you can pour into your own pans.

    M. Graham is also my favorite gouache. I use the white gouache to highlight my paintings.

  • Technical Pens

    • I use India ink pens often to finish my paintings. I also enjoy using these for line drawings, and they come in a variety of nib sizes and colors.

    My favorite pens: Faber-Castell Pitt Artist Pens, Prismacolor Premier Illustration Markers
