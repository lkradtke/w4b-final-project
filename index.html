    A few of my favorite supplies

  • Brushes
    • Princeton Aqua Elite

    These brushes are made specifically to hold water. They are made from a synthetic hair that mimics natural Kolinsky. These are professional level brushes that are affordable

    Where to buy: Dick Blick Art Materials, Jerry's Artarama

  • Paper

    • High quality watercolor paper is best because it allows the paint to be manipulated without putting stress on the paper. This includes blending, lifting, layering, etc. Choosing a 100% cotton watercolor paper is the best way to ensure your paints look their best.

    There are several ways to purchase paper. It can be purchased in the form of a sketchbook, loose or a watercolor block. A watercolor block is a pad of paper glued on all sides with a corner or a small section unglued so you can remove the paper when your painting is finished.

    I use: Fabriano Artistico, Legion Stonehenge

  • Paint
    • M. Graham

    M. Graham watercolors are my absolute favorite. The formula is highly pigmented and is very smooth. There is a wide range of colors to choose from, and lots of granulating options. The honey-based formula creates a paint that blends and mixes beautifully. The paints are easily rewetted and come in tubes that you can pour into your own pans.

    M. Graham
